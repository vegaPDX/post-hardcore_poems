Theme Comparison

1 — Weathered Parchment
Classics in the Key of Post-Hardcore

I never understood poetry
until I heard it.

In high school English, poetry was boring. Stories about people living in the 1700s and 1800s that I had no connection to. Then I started turning these poems into post-hardcore songs with modernized lyrics.

The Old Lie
from “Dulce et Decorum Est” — Wilfred Owen, 1920
Owen watched a man drown in poison gas in 1917 and dared anyone to repeat the lie that it is sweet and fitting to die for your country.
Marcus Has My Pen
from “The Destruction of Sennacherib” — Lord Byron, 1815
Byron showed an army alive in one stanza and dead in the next, never describing the killing. This song does the same thing with a school shooting.
Original Poem

Bent double, like old beggars under sacks, Knock-kneed, coughing like hags, we cursed through sludge, Till on the haunting flares we turned our backs, And towards our distant rest began to trudge.

All source poems are in the public domain.
